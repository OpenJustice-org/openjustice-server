# OpenJustice Project Governance

This document describes the governance model for the OpenJustice project. OpenJustice is a community-driven open source project licensed under the MIT License.

## Project Leadership

**Founding Maintainer:** Saidu Bundu-Kamara (bundukamarasaidu@gmail.com)

The founding maintainer holds final decision-making authority during the early stages of the project. As the project grows, co-maintainers will be added to distribute leadership responsibilities. The founding maintainer may step back from active leadership at any time by appointing a successor through consensus with the existing maintainer group.

### Adding Co-Maintainers

New co-maintainers are added through the process described in "How to Become a Maintainer" below. The goal is to build a maintainer team that reflects the breadth of the project and its contributor community.

## Decision Making

Decisions are made by **consensus among maintainers**. Consensus means that no maintainer raises a sustained objection after reasonable discussion.

- Routine decisions (bug fixes, minor improvements, documentation updates) can be merged by any maintainer.
- Significant decisions (new features, architectural changes, policy changes) should be discussed in a public issue or pull request and require consensus among maintainers.
- If consensus cannot be reached after good-faith discussion, a **simple majority vote** among maintainers is the fallback. Each maintainer gets one vote. In the event of a tie, the founding maintainer casts the deciding vote.

All significant decisions and their rationale should be documented in the relevant issue or pull request for transparency.

## Contributor Ladder

OpenJustice recognizes three levels of involvement:

### Contributor

Anyone who contributes to the project in any form -- code, documentation, bug reports, reviews, community support, or design.

**Criteria:**
- Submit at least one accepted contribution (pull request, issue, or similar).

**Privileges:**
- Listed in project acknowledgments.
- May participate in discussions and propose changes.

### Committer

A trusted contributor with write access to the repository.

**Criteria:**
- A track record of multiple quality contributions over a sustained period (typically three months or more).
- Demonstrated understanding of the project's goals, code standards, and review process.
- Nominated by an existing maintainer or committer.
- Approved by consensus of the maintainers.

**Privileges:**
- Write access to the repository.
- Ability to merge pull requests for routine changes.
- Responsibility to review contributions from others.

### Maintainer

A committer who takes on project-wide leadership and decision-making responsibilities.

**Criteria:**
- See "How to Become a Maintainer" below.

**Privileges:**
- Participation in all project decisions.
- Authority to make or approve significant changes.
- Responsibility for project direction, releases, and community health.

## How to Become a Maintainer

Becoming a maintainer is based on demonstrated commitment and trust.

**Requirements:**
1. **Sustained contributions** -- A consistent record of high-quality contributions over a significant period (typically six months or more), including code, reviews, documentation, or community work.
2. **Good judgment** -- Demonstrated ability to make sound technical and community decisions, handle disagreements constructively, and uphold the project's values.
3. **Nomination** -- An existing maintainer must nominate the candidate, providing specific examples of the candidate's contributions and readiness.
4. **Approval** -- The nomination is discussed privately among maintainers and approved by consensus.

The candidate is then invited to accept the role. Maintainer status can be voluntarily relinquished at any time. It can also be revoked through the conflict resolution or Code of Conduct enforcement processes described below.

## Conflict Resolution

Disagreements are a normal part of collaborative work. OpenJustice follows a graduated approach to resolving conflicts:

1. **Direct discussion.** Parties involved should first attempt to resolve the issue through respectful, direct communication (in the relevant issue, pull request, or private message).
2. **Escalation to maintainers.** If direct discussion does not resolve the conflict, any party may bring the matter to the maintainer group for mediation. Maintainers will review the situation and work toward a fair resolution.
3. **Code of Conduct committee.** If the conflict involves a violation of the Code of Conduct, or if the maintainer group cannot resolve it, the matter is escalated to the Code of Conduct committee as described in CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md.

All parties are expected to engage in good faith at every stage.

## Code of Conduct Enforcement

All participants in the OpenJustice project are expected to follow the project's Code of Conduct, documented in [CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

Reports of Code of Conduct violations can be sent to bundukamarasaidu@gmail.com. The maintainer group (or a designated Code of Conduct committee, once established) is responsible for reviewing reports, investigating incidents, and taking appropriate action. Actions may include warnings, temporary bans, permanent bans, or revocation of committer or maintainer status.

All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality to the extent possible.

## Changes to Governance

Amendments to this governance document require **consensus of all current maintainers**. Proposed changes should be submitted as a pull request and discussed openly before approval.
